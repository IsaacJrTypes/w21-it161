Accessibility

Accessibility implementation in web design brings equality and aid in creating better user experiences for everyone. The U.S. Department of Health and Human Services reported 1 in 4 U.S. adults identified as having a disability (Okoro, et al ). If a modern website does not develop elements of accessibility, they are discounting a large U.S. population.

The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), a 1990 landmark civil rights act, made it illegal for businesses to discriminate against people with disabilities in a public setting (ADA Title III). Kris Rivenburgh is an attorney and founder of Accessible.org wrote about the legal implications of websites that are not accessibility friendly. He writes “If you have a website and there’s anything remotely commercial about it, you’re a potential target for plaintiffs’ law firms”(The ADA Checklist). The act is the reason you see accessible parking spaces in front of businesses or ramps next to steps at the entrance of a building. Unlike ADA compliant business locations can be costly, there are techniques and tools that can help create an ADA compliant website.

When building a website with accessibility features in mind, there are techniques to help build an ADA friendly website. Accessible Rich Internet Applications (ARIA) is a web standard created to enhance HTML language for screen readers. Everyone will experience a decline in visual acuity within their lifetime. Studies have shown that as humans age, our contrast sensitivity declines (Salvi, et al). Pablo Standley, a designer, writes “The great thing about making our work accessible is that it brings a better experience to everyone” in a blog post titled Designing for Accessibility Is Not That Hard. He points out how adding high contrast ratio and texture to colors when communicating information can enrich the web experience for everybody.

During the Covid-19 pandemic I, like the rest of society, used the internet to learn, experience and communicate with others to make quarantine less lonely. People with impairments are no different. They have the same goals and dreams as everyone else. By applying accessibility in the developmental stage of web design, we help people with impairments feel welcomed to visit our digital doorsteps.
